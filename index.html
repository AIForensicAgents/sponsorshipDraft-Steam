AI Forensic Agents

Human-Centered Investigation of Harms Perpetuated by Artificial Intelligence

Epistemic Framework

AI Forensic Agents constitutes an interdisciplinary research and service initiative dedicated to the systematic forensic investigation of harms engendered by artificial intelligence systems. Grounded in phenomenological and sociotechnical analytical paradigms, the initiative deploys human-centered methodological frameworks to interrogate the causal architectures underlying algorithmic malfeasance, bias propagation, and emergent systemic risks.

The investigative apparatus encompasses computational auditing, adversarial robustness evaluation, counterfactual causal inference, and participatory design methodologies — each calibrated to surface the latent mechanisms through which AI systems engender epistemic, material, and dignitary harms across heterogeneous populations. By foregrounding the lived experiences of affected communities, AI Forensic Agents reconstitutes forensic inquiry as an inherently normative and emancipatory praxis.

Methodological Contributions

The initiative advances a tripartite investigative methodology: (1) retrospective harm archaeology, wherein historical patterns of technological failure are systematically excavated and taxonomized; (2) prospective risk cartography, employing scenario-based modeling to anticipate emergent failure modes; and (3) participatory accountability frameworks that center the epistemic authority of communities disproportionately subjected to algorithmic governance.

This methodological triangulation yields forensic narratives of unprecedented granularity, enabling stakeholders — from regulatory bodies to civil society organizations — to operationalize evidence-based interventions that mitigate the proliferation of AI-mediated harms while preserving the transformative potential of computational intelligence.
